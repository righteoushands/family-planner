Read claud.md first. Build the Rule of Life Wizard — Phase 1 of the Sancta Familia expansion. Plan before building. Do not start until the plan is written and presented.
What and why: The Rule of Life Wizard helps any family build their daily and weekly rhythm. It is runable anytime — first setup, seasonal changes, new school year. It has two modes: Lucy-guided (conversational, requires API key) and structured (form-based, no API key needed). It auto-saves after every step and resumes if interrupted.
New files needed: render_frol_wizard.py, data/frol_wizard_progress.json. New routes: /frol-wizard GET and POST, /frol-wizard-chat POST for Lucy mode. Add to More sheet navigation and create a “Build your Rule of Life” setup card on Lauren’s dashboard.
The wizard has 10 steps. Auto-saves to data/frol_wizard_progress.json after every step so interruptions lose nothing.
LANDING SCREEN: Beautiful full-screen intro. Text: “Your Rule of Life is the rhythm that holds your family’s day. It’s not a rigid schedule — it’s a framework of love. Let’s build yours together.” Two buttons: “Guide me with Lucy” (requires API key, checks os.environ for ANTHROPIC_API_KEY before showing this option) and “I’ll set it up myself.”
STEP 1 — Your Family: Confirm or collect family members. Name and role required. Birthday and color optional. Skip entirely if family members already exist in app_settings.json.
STEP 2 — Anchors: Wake time for school days and weekends per age group. Bedtime per age group. Fixed external commitments — recurring classes, jobs, appointments that happen same time every week.
STEP 3 — Prayer: Morning prayer time and which prayer (Morning Offering, Lauds, Rosary, other). Angelus — which times to observe (6 AM, noon, 6 PM). Divine Mercy at 3 PM yes/no. Evening — Rosary time and Vespers yes/no. Night — Compline yes/no and examination of conscience yes/no. Other family devotions free text. Sister Mary introduced here with “Meet her later” button linking to /sister-mary.
STEP 4 — Meals: Breakfast, lunch, dinner times. Who prepares each meal. Lunch together or separate. Morning prep for dinner yes/no. Batch cooking day of week. Lorenzo introduced here with “Meet him later” button linking to /lorenzo.
STEP 5 — Work Blocks: Homeschool yes/no. If yes — hours and which children. Basic subject list per child (details come later in curriculum setup). Chore time — morning, afternoon, or after school. Parent work from home hours to protect as blocked time. Father Gregory introduced here with “Meet him later” button linking to /gregory.
STEP 6 — Exercise and Health: Family exercise routine yes/no. When — morning, afternoon, evening. Who exercises. Types — strength, cardio, walks, PE, sports. Recurring classes or scheduled workouts. Family exercise time or individual. Coach introduced here with “Meet him later” button linking to /programs.
STEP 7 — Rest, Family, and Marriage: Afternoon rest or quiet time — who and when. Family free time — when. Weekly family traditions — movie night, game night, read-aloud (checkboxes with free text option). Date night — day and time. Daily couple time — morning coffee or evening together. Weekly marriage check-in day and time. Weekend rhythm differences from weekdays. Dr. Monica introduced here with “Meet her later” button linking to /dr-monica.
STEP 8 — Family Health and Wellbeing: Which family members to set up health tracking for. Any current medications (basic list — full prescription scanning comes in Phase 2). Recurring health appointments. Preferred notification channels — email, push, SMS, in-app — all optional, all off by default, each with individual toggle. Phone number and email for notifications. Emergency alert setup — optional section: up to 5 emergency contacts with name, phone, email. Pre-written emergency message (editable). Include location yes/no. Send via SMS, email, or both. Button placement — homepage, health section, or both.
STEP 9 — Each Person’s Role: For each family member show a visual summary of their day based on all previous steps. Simple time-block view. Allow adjustments. In Lucy mode she summarizes what she heard and asks confirmation per person. In structured mode show a time-slot editor per person. This step writes the actual data to data/day_templates/{Weekday}.json in the exact format the existing FROL system expects.
STEP 10 — Review: Full family week as a grid — all members, all 7 days. Highlight any obvious gaps (no meal prep time, prayer time missing). Allow edits. Save button. Closing message: “Your Rule of Life is saved. It will evolve — come back anytime to adjust it as your family grows and seasons change.”
LUCY MODE: In Lucy mode each step shows a chat interface alongside the form. Lucy asks conversational questions and fills in the form fields as the user answers. Use the existing Lucy chat pattern from render_lucy.py. Lucy’s responses are warm and encouraging. She notices patterns and makes gentle suggestions — for example if the family has young children she might suggest a longer afternoon rest block.
SAVING: After every step POST saves current progress to data/frol_wizard_progress.json with shape: current_step, mode (lucy or structured), completed_steps list, and all collected data so far. On GET /frol-wizard check if progress exists and resume from current_step.
ON COMPLETION: Write collected data to the appropriate existing data files — day_templates for the schedule, app_settings for family preferences, prayer_intentions for prayer schedule. Show a beautiful completion screen.
DESIGN: Beautiful, calm, encouraging throughout. Marian blue and warm cream palette matching the time-block homepage. Progress indicator showing which step the user is on. Each step has a title, a brief encouraging subtitle, and the form or chat interface. Back button on every step. Save and continue button at bottom.
