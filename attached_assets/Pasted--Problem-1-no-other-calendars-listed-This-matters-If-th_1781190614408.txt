

Problem 1 — no other calendars listed. This matters. If the app’s calendar settings page only shows the one calendar you just added, then either (a) the other calendar subscriptions were never actually stored where the settings page reads from, or (b) the settings page only reads from one of the two storage locations. Remember the June 1 audit found competing storage in several places — it’s plausible the subscribed-calendar config and the cache are out of sync, or there are two different lists. This is worth diagnosing because if the settings page can’t see existing subscriptions, you can’t manage them.

Problem 2 — CAP still not showing. This one might not be a bug at all, and I want to flag the most likely innocent explanation before we treat it as broken: Proton’s feed refresh lag. From the search: Proton subscribed calendars refresh every 8–16 hours, and the app has its own cache on top of that (last fetched June 8). So the chain is — your CAP event → Proton publishes it to the feed (up to 16 hrs) → the app fetches the feed into its cache (on whatever schedule the app runs). If you just created the link minutes ago, CAP almost certainly hasn’t propagated yet through either layer. It may simply need time.

But there’s also a real possibility it’s a genuine bug — the app may not be re-fetching the feed at all, which would explain why the cache has been stuck since June 8.
