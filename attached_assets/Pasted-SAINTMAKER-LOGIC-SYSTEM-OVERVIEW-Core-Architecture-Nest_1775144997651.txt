SAINTMAKER LOGIC — SYSTEM OVERVIEW
Core Architecture: Nested Review Cycles
Four nested time horizons: Annual → Seasonal (13 weeks) → Monthly → Weekly → Daily. Each level has a review/plan pair. Logic flows: reflect on past → discern → plan forward.
ANNUAL LEVEL
Goal list with: Title, Description, Assigned Season. Master orientation list for the whole year.
SEASONAL LEVEL (primary planning unit)
SEASONAL JOURNAL (start of season):
Goal Review: rate up to 5 goals by % done, keep or cancel. Reflection on main events/themes. Life Domains radar: 8 spokes rated 1-5 (Work, Finances, Health, Family & Friends, Intimacy, Spiritual Growth, Leisure, Environment). Blessings / Shortcomings. Discernment: Start / Stop / Change columns. Prayer Examination: checklist (Dry, Mechanistic, Tired, Infrequent, Distracted, Complicated, Slothful, Obligatory, Depressed, Clear, Fervent, Joyous, Expectant, Simple, Grateful, Humble, Continuous, Loving). Prayer Adjustments (free write).
SEASONAL PLANNING (after review):
Weekly Rule of Life: time-blocked grid, Sun-Sat x 6am-5am, canonical hours labeled (Prime, Terce, Sext, None, Vespers, Compline, Matins, Lauds). Goal Plans: up to 5 goals each with Summary, Life Domain category, Motivations x3, Milestones x3 with due dates, Task lists per milestone (3 tasks each). Seasonal Preview: 3-month calendar grid for big-picture event placement.
MONTHLY LEVEL
MONTH REVIEW:
After Action Review: rate Feast Plan / Prayer Plan / Goal Plan (1-10). Blessings / Shortcomings / Lessons Learned / Discernment (what is God calling you to next month?). Month Checklist: Complete AAR, Describe Blessings/Shortcomings/Lessons/Discernment, Make Feast Plan, Make Prayer Plan, Create Goal Plan, Add dates/feasts to calendar, Add events/appointments to calendar, Set up Virtue Tracker (optional).
MONTH PLAN:
Feast Plan: up to 3 feasts with devotion notes and dates. Prayer Plan: Morning / Afternoon / Evening columns (free write). Goal Plan: progress on up to 5 seasonal goals (dot grid).
WEEKLY LEVEL
WEEKLY EXAMINATION / OVERVIEW:
Stewardship: Were you a good steward of God’s gifts? God’s Will: Did you do God’s will or your own? Prayer: Did you say your prayers with care? Gratitude: Were you grateful to God for His gifts? Duties: Did you do your duties in work, family life, community life, and to God? Mass Intention: Set an intention for Sunday Mass.
WEEKLY 3 + WEEK PLAN:
Three priority items (Weekly 3). Four free-write quadrants: Domestic / Recreation / Vocation / Spiritual. Weeks remaining tracker: 13 down to 0. Blessings: In what ways did God bless your life and work this week? Improvements: Review Weekly 3 and goals. How could you improve next week?
DAILY LEVEL
LEFT PAGE (planning/doing):
Date + Feast/Event header. Gratitude: What are you grateful to God for? Devotions: FA/FE column and CA/PA column, 3 slots each. Daily Cross: What challenge can you offer up to God? To-Do List: # priority, ABC ranking, Task, Status (checkmark=Completed, O=Delegated, arrow=Shifted to tomorrow, X=Canceled).
RIGHT PAGE (appointments + reflection):
Appointments: time-blocked 6am-11pm with canonical hours marked (Prime 6am, Terce 9am, Sext 12pm, None 2pm, Vespers 5pm, Compline 8pm, Matins midnight, Lauds 3am). Meditation Journal: Reflection (spend 10 min meditating on a holy subject), Resolution (spiritual resolution to keep). Evening Examen: Blessings x3 (how did God bless your life today?), Examen (was your heart with God today? why or why not?), Resolution (practical step to improve tomorrow). Notes (dot grid).
KEY DESIGN PATTERNS
Canonical hours appear at daily, weekly, and seasonal layers — structural anchors not decoration. “Offer up” framing and Mass intention are distinctly Catholic spiritual logic. Life Domains radar recurs seasonally as a balance check against goal tunnel vision. Week is the primary operational unit. 13-week season is the main planning container. Year is oriented around liturgical time, not calendar quarters. FA/FE = Family Activity / Family Evening (or Feast Activity / Feast Evening). CA/PA = likely Catechesis/Prayer Activity variants (personal devotions). Status symbols on tasks allow forward momentum without deletion: nothing is “failed,” only redirected.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
